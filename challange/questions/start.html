Moving to a new country is both exciting and challenging. For those newly arriving to Switzerland, it is immediately noticeable how different the systems and opportunities are here. Switzerland is often described as a country of high living standards and fair economic opportunities. But as foreigners, our experiences prompt us to ask: Are these opportunities truly equal for everyone?

Education, employment, and property ownership have been important factors to understand how quality of life is shaped.

We wanted to delve deeper and explore how social differences, particularly for foreign nationals, influence on accessing these opportunities. In this article, we will address key questions such as:

Does higher education genuinely lead to more comfortable life?

Do foreigners in Switzerland have the same chances for employment and property ownership as citizens?

By exploring these questions, we aim to provide a perspective that blends emotion and objectivity. This is not merely an academic inquiry but a reflection of the shared experiences of our team and countless others striving to build a new life here.
